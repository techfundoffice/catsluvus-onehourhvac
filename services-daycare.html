Cat Daycare Services

Does your cat spend long hours home alone? Are you worried about boredom, loneliness, or destructive behavior while you’re at work? Cats Luv Us Cat Daycare provides a stimulating, supervised daytime environment where your cat can socialize, play, explore, and rest — all under the watchful care of our trained cat care specialists.

Our daycare program is designed for cats who thrive with company, enjoy interactive play, or simply need more stimulation than an empty house can provide. It’s also an excellent option for kittens who need socialization, post-surgical cats who need monitoring while you’re at work, or senior cats who benefit from daily wellness checks and gentle activity.

A Day at Cats Luv Us Daycare

When your cat arrives in the morning, they’ll be welcomed by our team and settled into the daycare area, which features climbing structures, window perches, interactive toys, hiding spots, and comfortable resting nooks. Throughout the day, our specialists engage cats in structured play sessions using feather wands, laser pointers, puzzle feeders, and other enrichment tools that stimulate natural hunting instincts and promote physical exercise.

Cats who prefer solo activities have access to quiet zones with individual perches and cozy beds. We understand that not every cat is a social butterfly, and we respect each cat’s individual comfort level. All cats in our daycare program must pass a temperament assessment to ensure they are comfortable in a shared environment.

Daily Schedule

  • 7:30 AM – 9:00 AM: Drop-off window & morning check-in
  • 9:00 AM – 10:30 AM: Morning play session & enrichment
  • 10:30 AM – 12:00 PM: Rest time & window watching
  • 12:00 PM – 12:30 PM: Midday snack & water refresh
  • 12:30 PM – 2:00 PM: Afternoon play session
  • 2:00 PM – 4:00 PM: Quiet time & individual attention
  • 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM: Pick-up window

Requirements

All daycare cats must be current on FVRCP and rabies vaccinations, spayed or neutered (for cats 6 months and older), free of fleas and parasites, and in general good health. A temperament evaluation is required before the first daycare session to ensure your cat is comfortable in a shared environment. Cats who show signs of aggression or extreme stress in social settings may be better suited for individual boarding or in-home pet sitting.
