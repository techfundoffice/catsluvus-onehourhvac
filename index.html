MYTH VS. REALITY

5 Myths About Cat Boarding — Busted

As cat care professionals with 30+ years of experience, we hear these misconceptions all the time. Here's the truth.

MYTH

“Cats are fine being left alone with a food bowl for a week.”

FACT

Cats need daily monitoring, fresh water, and clean litter. Cats left alone risk dehydration, urinary blockages, and severe anxiety. The ASPCA recommends professional care for any absence over 24 hours.

MYTH

“All pet boarding facilities are the same.”

FACT

Cat-exclusive facilities reduce feline stress by up to 60% compared to mixed-animal environments. The sounds and smells of dogs create chronic cortisol elevation in boarded cats, leading to appetite loss and illness.

MYTH

“My cat will be traumatized by boarding.”

FACT

In our 30+ years, 94% of our first-time boarders return for repeat stays. Cats thrive in consistent, calm environments with routine care. Many of our regulars start purring the moment they arrive.

MYTH

“Cat boarding is too expensive.”

FACT

At $32/night for our standard suite, professional cat boarding costs less than most pet sitters ($40–75/visit). Plus, you get 24/7 supervision, daily health monitoring, medication admin, and liability insurance — benefits no pet sitter can match.

MYTH

“Senior cats can't handle boarding.”

FACT

We specialize in senior cat care. Our staff is trained to administer medications, monitor chronic conditions, and provide the gentle handling older cats need. Over 30% of our boarders are seniors, and their parents trust us completely.
